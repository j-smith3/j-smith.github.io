Consider the Usage

     What type of activities will you be doing with your bicycle? Do you plan to just ride paved trails? Or is off road more of your thing? There are a variety of categories that can dictate just what type of bicycle you should target. A few uses to consider are the following:

  • Do you plan to ride off-road, in hilly areas with possibly steep inclines?
  • Do you plan to do most of your riding on paved surfaces and/or want a bike built for speed?
  • Do you plan to just casually ride, taking the scenic route?

     Whatever you plan to do the most with your bike should dictate what type of bike and accessories
you will need.

Physical Features

     We all come in a variety of shapes and sizes and obviously not every bike will fit every physique. Make sure that the bike you choose is one that is a custom fit for you. Whether it is a taller framed bike, or a low rider, find one that fits your height.

     Handlebars can also affect your comfort level while riding. Make sure your ride is comfortable for you and you are not having to contort in awkward positions just to go for a ride.

     Additional features can also be a matter of preference. For instance, your choice of brakes (pedal or handle), the number of available gears to interchange, or the suspension; these can all have an impact on what bike is right for you.


Possible Accessories

Additional accessories are usually a matter of preference but can really improve the enjoyment of your ride. Bikes are incredibly customizable, and the following areas are just some basic examples of modifications that you can choose.

  • Brakes: There are multiple types, but it boils down to do you want to stop with a level grip for your hand or using the pedals and your legs.
  • Gears: The gear box on a bike can either be a single gear, or it can have dozens of gears. Adjusting the gear level changes the intensity and resistance of the act of pedaling. Mountain bikes usually benefit from having the ability to lessen the pedal resistance on steep inclines.
  • Suspension/Tires: As with cars, different forms of suspension and types of tires can affect the
    overall performance and handling of a bike. Depending on the intended activity, customization is
    a must.


Take a Test Ride

The best way to find out what you like in a bike, or if a certain bike is the one for you, is to take it for a test ride. You know what you like in a bike ride and what performance you hope to achieve, so take a test ride and find out if it is right for you. Consider characteristics like how the bike handles the terrain, if the frame feels sturdy and supportive, or if the ride is comfortable. It never hurts to try it out before you invest.
